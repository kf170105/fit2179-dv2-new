Malaysia faces a persistent challenge in maintaining
adequate blood supplies. Malaysia requires over 2,000
bags of blood daily, yet only 2.2% to 2.3% of the
population donates annually. This dashboard explores
patterns hidden in donation records from geographic
disparities to seasonal trends and donor loyalty to
understand how Malaysia can strengthen its blood
donation culture.

Section 2 — Where do Malaysians donate?

Kuala Lumpur leads with 567 average daily donors

Blood donation rates vary significantly across Malaysian states. Peninsular states show higher donation rates than East Malaysia. Darker red = higher donation activity per day.


Geographic inequality suggests targeted campaigns in lower-donation states could help ensure equitable blood supply across Malaysia.

Section 3 — When do Malaysians donate?

W.P. KL averages 567 donors per day
which is 4 times more than any other state

Donation rates follow a seasonal pattern — peaks occur mid-year across most states. Some states show strong consistency while others fluctuate significantly by month.


Blood banks can predict high-demand months and plan campaigns during low periods to stabilise supply throughout the year.

Section 4 — Who donates blood?

Civilians account for over 85% of all donations year-round

Students spike during blood donation campaigns
while police and army contributions remain steady
but small throughout the year.

Section 5 — Trends and donor loyalty

Regular donors rise from 46.9 to 47.8 per hospital per

10 of 12 states grew in 2025 sustaining the momentum
