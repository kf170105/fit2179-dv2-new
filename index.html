Malaysia faces a persistent challenge in maintaining adequate blood supplies. This dashboard explores patterns hidden in donation records — from geographic disparities to seasonal trends and donor loyalty — to understand how Malaysia can strengthen its blood donation culture.

Source: Ministry of Health Malaysia (PDN) via data.gov.my

Which Malaysian State Donates the Most Blood?

Perak leads with ~129 avg. daily donors — highest in Malaysia

Blood donation rates vary significantly across Malaysian states. Peninsular states generally show higher donation rates than East Malaysia. Darker red = higher donation activity per day.


Geographic inequality suggests targeted campaigns in lower-donation states could help ensure equitable blood supply across Malaysia.

Section 3 — When do Malaysians donate?

W.P. KL averages 560 donors per day — 4× more than any other state

Donation rates follow a seasonal pattern — peaks occur mid-year across most states. Some states show strong consistency while others fluctuate significantly by month.


Blood banks can predict high-demand months and plan donation campaigns during low periods to stabilise supply throughout the year.

Are Malaysians Becoming Regular Donors?

Regular donors rose 46.9 → 47.8 per hospital per day in 2024

All donor types increased in 2024. Regular donors show the strongest growth, signalling a maturing donor culture in Malaysia.

Which States Are Improving Donations?

Kelantan improved +13.3% — most improved state in 2024

Most states improved from 2023 to 2024. Kedah and Perak declined slightly — targeted campaigns could help reverse the trend.
