Malaysia faces a persistent challenge in maintaining
adequate blood supplies. Malaysia requires over 2,000
bags of blood daily, yet only 2.2% to 2.3% of the
population donates annually. This dashboard explores
patterns hidden in donation records from geographic
disparities to seasonal trends and donor loyalty to
understand how Malaysia can strengthen its blood
donation culture.

Section 2 — Where do Malaysians Donate?

Blood donation patterns vary dramatically across
Malaysia's 13 states — geography, population density
and hospital access shape who gives and how often.

Kuala Lumpur leads with 567 average daily donors
between 2023 and 2025 which is the highest
in Malaysia.
Peninsular states record higher donations than East
Malaysia — geographic inequality calls for targeted
campaigns nationwide.
Pusat Darah Negara in West Malaysia leads with 690,000+
total donors between 2023 and 2025 as it is Malaysia's
only national blood hub, it centralises
supply for all hospitals nationwide.
East Malaysia's 6 hospitals collect far
fewer donations as distance and limited
access create a critical supply gap.

Section 3 — When do Malaysians Donate?

Beyond geography, timing matters. Seasonal rhythms
reveal when Malaysians donate — giving blood banks
the power to plan campaigns and prevent shortages.

Donation levels stay relatively uniform across states,
except for Kuala Lumpur(main capital) averages 567 donors per day and 624 donors in May which are 4 times more
than any other state.
Mid-year peaks reveal seasonal giving patterns —
blood banks should fill campaigns to fill gaps
in low-donation months for stable supply.
Type O tops 40% of daily donations meaning most
Malaysians carry O blood naturally. Type A
holds 30%, Type B shows steady donation pattern
throughout the years. Type AB is under 5%
which is the rarest yet critical for rare patients.

Section 4 — Who Donates Blood?

Timing patterns are driven by who donates. Malaysia's
donor base spans civilians, students and uniformed
personnel— each group contributing differently.

Civilians account for over 85% of all donations year-round
Students spike during blood donation campaigns
while police and army contributions remain steady
but small throughout the years.

Section 5 — The Outcomes: Trends and Donor Loyalty

Understanding who donates raises a bigger question —
is Malaysia building a loyal, sustainable donor
culture that grows stronger every year?

Regular donors rise from 46.9 to 47.8 per hospital per
day in 2024, with more Malaysians giving blood
habitually between 2023 and 2024.

New and irregular donors declined in 2024 — fewer first-timers
as loyal regulars dominate, suggesting campaigns retain better
than recruit.
9 of 12 states grew in 2025 sustaining the momentum
built in 2024, proving Malaysia's donor growth is a
consistent trend, not a one-year spike.
Kelantan improved +13.3% — most improved state in 2024
North states(Kedah & Perak) slip and outreach is needed.
KL dominates rank 1 due to urban hospitals and densest population.
Overall stable state rankings reflect population density pattern.
Most hospitals included many East Malaysia hospitals
remain in Develop Zone due to geographic barriers
restricting donor access, while Pusat Darah Negara's
total 568 donors/day centralises national supply.
